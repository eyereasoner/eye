Reason why
==========
Langtons Ant evolves on an infinite grid of black/white cells.
At each step the ant:
  1) Looks at the colour of its current cell.
  2) Turns 90 right on white, 90 left on black.
  3) Flips the colour of the current cell.
  4) Moves forward one cell.
These two simple rules yield chaotic transients followed by a
highwaya repeating pattern that drifts off to infinity.

Check (harness)
===============
Forward then reverse (1 step) recovers exact state? False
Forward 2000 then reverse 2000 returns to initial state? False
Parity of black-cell count matches step index (from 0)? True
Deterministic evolution (same steps  same state)? True

All checks passed? False
