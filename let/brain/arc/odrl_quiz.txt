Reason why
==========
We flatten each rule (permission/prohibition/obligation) to a tuple:
  ⟨assignee, action, target, [Expr,…]⟩  with Expr ∈ {Atom | Or | And | Xone | AndSeq}.
Each rule expands to disjunctive branches (clauses) grouped by leftOperand.
Two rules are comparable only if they share scope (same assignee and target)
and their actions overlap via equality or odrl:includedIn*/rdfs:subClassOf*.
For each Permission/Obligation vs Prohibition pair we check branches:
  • Overlap: all shared operands are jointly satisfiable;
  • Ambiguity: there exists a value v on a shared operand that satisfies P but not N.
If at least one pair overlaps and admits such a v ⇒ Ambiguous;
if at least one pair overlaps but none admit v ⇒ Conflict;
if no pair overlaps ⇒ No conflict.
