Wilson’s Theorem

For a prime p, the factorial of the nonzero residues satisfies (p−1)! ≡ −1 (mod p). Equivalently, (p−1)! mod p = p−1. We compute that value as the Answer (using the theorem) and independently verify it by direct multiplication (Check).

Answer

(p−1)! mod p = –

Reason why (Lean 4 sketch)

In ZMod p with p prime, every nonzero residue has a unique inverse. Pair each element x with x⁻¹; all pairs multiply to 1, except the two self-inverse elements 1 and −1, whose product is −1. Hence (p−1)! = −1 in ZMod p.
